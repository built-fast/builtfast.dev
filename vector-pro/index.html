Vector Pro is the API for the Vector platform, a serverless WordPress hosting infrastructure built by BuiltFast. It gives hosting partners everything they need to offer scalable, white-label WordPress hosting to their customers—without managing the underlying infrastructure.

Who Vector Pro is For

Vector Pro is designed for hosting companies, agencies, and product companies who want to offer WordPress hosting as part of their service. Rather than building and maintaining your own hosting stack, you integrate with Vector Pro and let BuiltFast handle the infrastructure while you focus on your customers.

Hosting Companies

Add a scalable WordPress tier to your offerings without infrastructure overhead

Agencies

Host client sites under your own brand with enterprise reliability

Product Companies

Theme/plugin developers and SaaS builders needing reliable hosting

What Vector Pro Provides

Through the API, partners can:

  • Create and manage sites — Provision new WordPress sites for your customers, each with its own development environment and deployable environments
  • Deploy to production — Push sites from development to staging or production with a single API call
  • Manage environments — Create additional environments (QA, client review, etc.) as needed
  • Handle custom domains — Attach customer domains to production sites with automatic SSL provisioning

All of this happens under your brand. Your customers see your domain, your branding—BuiltFast stays invisible.

The Key Differentiator

Vector sites scale to handle traffic spikes without manual intervention. When your customer's site gets featured on a major publication or runs a successful campaign, it just works—no emergency scaling, no firefighting, no 3am phone calls.

How It Works (The Short Version)

When you create a site through Vector Pro:

  1. A development environment is provisioned — This is where your customer does their WordPress work: installing plugins, customizing themes, creating content. They access WP Admin here and can upload files via SFTP.
  2. Environments are created but dormant — Each site comes with staging and production environments. These don't consume resources until something is deployed to them.
  3. Deploys push to serverless infrastructure — When ready, a deploy takes the current state of development and pushes it to the target environment. The deployed environment runs on serverless infrastructure that scales automatically.
  4. Production gets the full treatment — Production environments include CDN, WAF, DDoS protection, and SSL. Non-production environments run without CDN for cost efficiency.

This architecture means your customers get the familiar WordPress experience during development, with the reliability and scalability of serverless infrastructure in production.

What Makes This Different

Most WordPress hosting falls into two camps: traditional hosting that requires capacity planning and doesn't scale gracefully, or "managed" hosting that's really just traditional hosting with a support team. Neither solves the fundamental problem.

Vector takes a different approach. The serverless architecture means sites genuinely scale based on demand. Your customers pay for what they use, not for idle capacity. And when traffic spikes, the infrastructure responds—automatically, instantly, without intervention.

For partners, this means you can confidently sell hosting without worrying about infrastructure limits. For your customers, it means their sites stay up when it matters most.
