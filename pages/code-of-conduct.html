01

Tolerance

Participants will be tolerant of opposing views.

02

Respect

Participants must ensure that their language and actions are free of personal attacks and disparaging personal remarks.

03

Good Faith

When interpreting the words and actions of others, participants should always assume good intentions.

04

Zero Tolerance

Behaviour which can be reasonably considered harassment will not be tolerated.

// why this matters

Building software is a team sport.

Open source thrives when contributors feel safe to participate. A welcoming environment doesn't happen by accident—it requires intentional effort from everyone involved.

These guidelines aren't bureaucratic box-checking. They're the foundation that lets us focus on what matters: building useful tools, sharing knowledge, and helping each other improve.

Whether you're opening your first issue or maintaining a project, we're glad you're here.

Adapted from the Ruby Community Conduct Guideline.
